Covid-19 and NPI, joint with the Global Policy Lab [link]

Societies around the world are weighing whether the health benefits of policies designed to slow the spread of COVID-19 are worth their very real social and economic costs. Many of these costs are plainly seen; for example, 36 million Americans have filed for unemployment over the last 2 months. But the health benefits of anti-contagion policies – in the form of infections that have been avoided – are unseen. Our objective was to measure the health benefits of these policies.
